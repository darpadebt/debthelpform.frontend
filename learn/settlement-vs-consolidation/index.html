How debt settlement and consolidation loans differ

Both options are designed to help you pay off unsecured debt, but they work in very different ways. A debt consolidation loan replaces multiple cards with one fixed loan payment. Debt settlement negotiates enrolled accounts to resolve for less than the full balance over time.

Debt consolidation loan
One new loan with a fixed term and interest rate, often used to simplify monthly payments.
Debt settlement program
Negotiates balances for less than owed, with payments based on the amount set aside each month.
Ideal for
Consolidation: steady income and credit that can qualify for a loan. Settlement: hardship and high balances where minimums feel out of reach.
Credit impact
Consolidation: hard inquiry and on‚Äëtime payments can help over time. Settlement: scores may dip during negotiations before recovering.

Debt consolidation loan highlights

A consolidation loan can lower interest rates and simplify payments when your credit and income qualify.

  • One predictable payment each month
  • Potentially lower APR than credit cards
  • No negotiation with creditors

Debt settlement highlights

Settlement focuses on resolving balances for less than owed, with a structured savings plan.

  • Payments based on your budget
  • Negotiated resolutions over time
  • Designed for people experiencing hardship
